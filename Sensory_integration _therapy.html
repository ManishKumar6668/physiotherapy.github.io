About Sensory Integration Theraphy

Sensory Integration Therapy (SIT) is a type of therapy that is used to help individuals who struggle with sensory processing issues. Sensory processing refers to how our brains receive and interpret information from our senses, such as touch, sound, sight, taste, and smell. When an individual has difficulty processing sensory information, it can impact their ability to perform everyday tasks and participate in social interactions.

SIT involves the use of specific activities and exercises to help an individual integrate and organize sensory input. The therapy is typically administered by an occupational therapist who is trained in SIT. The therapist will work with the individual to create a personalized treatment plan based on their specific needs and sensory challenges.

Some common activities used in SIT include swinging, climbing, jumping, and playing with various textures and materials. These activities are designed to stimulate the individual's sensory systems in a controlled and structured environment. Through repetitive exposure to sensory stimuli, the individual can learn to better process and integrate sensory information, leading to improvements in their overall functioning.

SIT is often used to help children with autism spectrum disorder, ADHD, and other sensory processing disorders, but it can also be beneficial for adults with similar challenges. It is important to note that SIT should always be administered by a trained occupational therapist and should be tailored to the individual's specific needs and abilities.
