About Social Skills Training

Social Skills Training (SST) is a type of therapy that aims to teach individuals specific skills and strategies to improve their social interactions and relationships. SST can be helpful for individuals with a range of social difficulties, including those with autism spectrum disorder, ADHD, anxiety disorders, and other mental health conditions.

SST typically involves both individual and group therapy sessions, where participants learn and practice social skills in a structured and supportive environment. The therapy can be tailored to the specific needs of the individual, but common social skills addressed in SST may include:

  • Effective communication (verbal and nonverbal)
  • Active listening
  • Identifying and expressing emotions
  • Problem-solving
  • Conflict resolution
  • Empathy and perspective-taking

During SST, participants may engage in role-playing exercises, group discussions, and other interactive activities to practice and reinforce social skills. The therapist may also provide feedback and guidance on how to improve communication and social interactions.

It is important to note that SST should always be administered by a trained mental health professional and should be tailored to the individual's specific needs and abilities. SST can be used alone or as part of a comprehensive treatment plan for individuals with social difficulties.
